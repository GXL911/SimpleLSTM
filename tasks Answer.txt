what is difference between pearson and spearman? How high is called high correlation?

Pearson: Linear relationships between measured variables
Spearman: Measure monotonic relationships and deal with nonlinear relationships
0.7 to 1.0 (or -0.7 to -1.0) for strong correlation
0.5 to 0.7 (or -0.5 to -0.7) for moderate correlation

 What is difference comparing to BFill and Ffill used in Python?

BFill (Backward Fill): Fills missing values with the next valid observation
FFill (Forward Fill): Fills missing values with the previous valid observation

what is difference between min-max scaler and standard scaler?

Min-Max Scaler: Scales features to a fixed range, typically 0 to 1
Standard Scaler: Standardizes features by removing mean and scaling to unit variance

why researchers dislike MAPE?
MAPE is like a not-so-fair referee. It is more tolerant of underestimation and harsher of overestimation. And, when the actual value is small or zero, it gives misleading results
